At FUEL, we believe recovery isn't just about the injury site — it's about the whole person. Our philosophy is grounded in the biopsychosocial model, meaning we treat your body, your thoughts, your emotions, your behaviours, and the social, economic, environmental, and cultural factors that influence your health.

That's not just a philosophy. It's how every practitioner at FUEL approaches every patient, every session.

Our Treatment
Approach

Evidence-informed, patient-centred, and always evolving. Here's what sets a FUEL treatment plan apart.

🔄

Dynamic Treatment Plans

Your care plan isn't static. Practitioners continuously reassess your progress and adapt your plan to match where you are in your recovery — not where you were when you first walked in.

🎯

Tailored Recovery Protocols

No two patients are the same. Exercises, stretches, and rehab strategies are personalized to your current ability level, lifestyle, and recovery goals — helping you make progress safely and confidently.

🤝

Interdisciplinary Collaboration

Practitioners at FUEL talk to each other about your case. That means when your osteopath identifies something that your massage therapist should know, it gets communicated — not siloed.

Frequently Asked
Questions

Interdisciplinary means our practitioners — chiropractors, osteopaths, massage therapists, acupuncturists, psychologists, and personal trainers — actively communicate and collaborate on your care plan. Rather than treating you in isolation, each practitioner contributes a piece of the puzzle. The result is faster, more effective recovery because your treatment is coordinated rather than fragmented.

No referral is needed to book an appointment at FUEL. Most of our services are direct-access, meaning you can book directly online through JaneApp or by calling our clinic at (780) 705-5452. If you're accessing care through MVA (Motor Vehicle Accident) insurance or a third-party insurer, we can help guide you through that process as well.

Your first visit begins with a thorough health history and assessment so your practitioner can understand not just your pain or injury, but the full context of your health. From there, they'll develop an initial treatment plan and — where appropriate — begin treatment in that same session. You'll also receive guidance on home exercises or lifestyle adjustments to support your recovery between sessions. We recommend wearing or bringing comfortable clothing.
