How Chiropractic Care at FUEL Works

Every patient's journey begins with a thorough assessment. Dr. Saša develops a treatment plan tailored to your specific goals, history, and lifestyle. Here's what to expect:

Step 1 — Initial Assessment

Comprehensive health history review, postural analysis, movement screening, and neurological testing to identify the root causes of your pain or dysfunction.

Step 2 — Targeted Adjustments

Precise, clinically-indicated musculoskeletal adjustments restore joint function and reduce nerve irritation. Adjustments are always explained and consented before treatment.

Step 3 — Ongoing Maintenance

A combination of manual therapy, rehabilitative exercise guidance, and lifestyle advice ensures lasting results and supports long-term spinal health.

Conditions We Treat

Dr. Saša's training in kinesiology, chiropractic, and sports medicine allows him to effectively address a wide range of musculoskeletal conditions:

  • Back pain (acute and chronic)
  • Neck pain and cervical dysfunction
  • Headaches and migraines
  • Joint pain (hips, knees, shoulders, wrists)
  • TMJ (temporomandibular joint) dysfunction
  • Seniors chiropractic care
  • Pediatric chiropractic
  • Motor vehicle accident injuries
  • Posture dysfunction and forward head carriage
  • Sciatica and nerve compression
  • Sports and repetitive strain injuries
  • Work-related musculoskeletal disorders

Meet Dr. Saša Babović

Dr. Saša Babović brings a uniquely well-rounded background to his chiropractic practice. With a BSc in Kinesiology from Simon Fraser University, a Doctor of Chiropractic designation, and a Master of Science in Sports Medicine from the University of Western States, his expertise spans biomechanics, injury rehabilitation, and athletic performance.

Before entering clinical practice, Dr. Saša worked as a youth sports coach, giving him an intuitive understanding of movement, coordination, and the demands placed on the body during sport and daily activity. He brings that same energy and attentiveness to every patient interaction at FUEL.
