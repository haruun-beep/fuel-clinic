Personal Training at FUEL

Exercise is medicine — and at FUEL, it's prescribed with the same precision and individualization as any other clinical therapy. Whether you're looking to build strength, lose weight, improve athletic performance, or recover from an injury, our certified trainer Jake Collier brings both the technical expertise and the motivational support to help you reach your goals safely and effectively.

Because personal training at FUEL happens within an interdisciplinary clinical environment, you have access to an integrated team. Jake works closely with our chiropractor, osteopath, and massage therapists — meaning your training program is always informed by your physical health status and recovery progress.

Three Pillars of Training at FUEL

Improve Strength

Structured, progressive resistance training programs designed to build functional strength, improve body composition, and develop proper movement mechanics. Every exercise is taught with attention to technique and injury prevention.

Whole Body Wellness

Training at FUEL addresses the full picture — strength, cardiovascular conditioning, flexibility, balance, and lifestyle habits. A truly well-rounded approach to long-term health and vitality.

Accountability & Motivation

Consistent check-ins, goal tracking, and the support of a certified trainer who genuinely invests in your progress. Having accountability makes all the difference in achieving lasting results.

Personal Training vs. Rehabilitative Exercise

FUEL offers two distinct but complementary forms of exercise-based care:

Personal Training

Goal-oriented fitness programming for individuals who are generally healthy and want to improve strength, fitness, body composition, or athletic performance. Ideal for prevention, performance, and lifestyle goals.

Rehabilitative Exercise

Therapeutic exercise programming specifically designed to restore function, reduce pain, and rebuild strength following injury, surgery, or a period of deconditioning. Closely coordinated with clinical practitioners.

Because Jake is also a Registered Massage Therapist (RMT), he can identify and address soft tissue restrictions that may be limiting your movement — making his sessions uniquely effective compared to personal training outside a clinical setting.

What to Expect in a Session

  • Movement screening and fitness assessment to establish your baseline
  • Goal-setting conversation to align training with your specific outcomes
  • Structured warm-up, main training block, and cool-down
  • Exercise instruction with real-time technique feedback
  • Progressive program adjustments based on your response and recovery
  • Lifestyle and nutrition guidance to support your training goals

Who Personal Training Is For

  • Individuals new to exercise looking for safe, guided introduction
  • Active adults wanting to build strength and improve performance
  • People recovering from injury, surgery, or chronic pain
  • Those who have plateaued with their current fitness routine
  • Patients completing other FUEL therapies who want to maintain and build on their progress
  • Seniors looking for safe, effective strength and balance programming

Jake's training philosophy centres on sustainable progress, movement quality, and building genuine confidence in your body. He meets every client where they are and builds from there — no judgment, no ego, just results.
