Psychology at FUEL

FUEL Psychology provides compassionate, evidence-based counselling support for individuals navigating both challenging life circumstances and positive personal growth. Led by Christina Worthing, our psychology services are offered within a safe, non-judgmental space where you can explore what's holding you back — and build the tools to move forward.

We believe in treating the whole person. Pain, stress, trauma, and mental health are not separate from the body — they are deeply intertwined. FUEL's interdisciplinary approach allows psychology to be meaningfully integrated alongside chiropractic, osteopathy, massage, and other physical therapies in a truly comprehensive care plan.

The biopsychosocial model — Pain is not purely physical. Biological factors, psychological states, and social circumstances all shape how we experience and recover from pain and injury. FUEL Psychology addresses all three dimensions.

Therapy Approaches

Christina is trained in several evidence-based therapeutic modalities and will draw on the approach best suited to your individual needs and goals:

ACT — Acceptance & Commitment Therapy

Helps you accept difficult thoughts and feelings rather than fighting them, while committing to actions aligned with your core values. Particularly effective for chronic pain and anxiety.

CBT — Cognitive Behavioural Therapy

Identifies and restructures unhelpful thought patterns and behaviours that contribute to distress, anxiety, depression, and pain catastrophizing.

Solution-Focused Therapy

A goal-oriented approach that focuses on your strengths and what's already working in your life — building solutions rather than dwelling on problems.

Emotion-Focused Therapy

Helps you identify, experience, and transform emotions that underlie psychological distress — improving emotional regulation and interpersonal wellbeing.

Frequently Asked Questions

Psychology services are not covered under the Alberta Health Care Insurance Plan (AHCIP) for most outpatient visits. However, many extended health benefit plans include psychology coverage. Additionally, some patients qualify for coverage through workplace insurance, WCB, or motor vehicle accident (MVA) claims. Contact your insurer to confirm your benefits, and our team will help you navigate the billing process.

The biopsychosocial model recognizes that health and illness are shaped by the interaction of biological factors (physiology, genetics, injury), psychological factors (thoughts, emotions, beliefs, coping strategies), and social factors (relationships, environment, culture, work). Rather than treating pain as a purely mechanical problem, this model looks at the whole person — which leads to more effective, sustainable recovery outcomes.

The duration of counselling depends entirely on your goals and circumstances. Some clients achieve their goals in 6–8 sessions; others benefit from longer-term support over many months. Christina will work collaboratively with you to establish realistic goals and review progress regularly. There is no pressure to continue beyond what feels right for you.
