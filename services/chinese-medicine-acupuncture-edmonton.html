Traditional Chinese Medicine at FUEL

Traditional Chinese Medicine (TCM) is a complete system of healthcare that views the body as an integrated whole — where physical health, mental wellbeing, and the flow of vital energy (Qi) are inseparably connected. When this balance is disrupted through stress, injury, poor diet, or illness, TCM practitioners use time-tested techniques to restore harmony and function.

At FUEL, our TCM practitioners offer a comprehensive suite of services tailored to your individual health presentation — from customized herbal formulas to acupuncture, cupping, auricular therapy, Tui Na massage, and moxibustion.

TCM Services Offered

Acupuncture

Sterile, hair-thin needles are inserted at specific meridian points to restore energy flow, reduce pain, and stimulate the body's natural healing responses.

Cupping Therapy

Suction cups applied to the skin create negative pressure, loosening connective tissue, promoting blood flow, and releasing deep muscular tension.

Auricular Therapy

Micro-needles or seeds applied to specific points on the outer ear to address pain, anxiety, addiction, and systemic conditions.

Tui Na Massage

Chinese medical massage combining rhythmic pressure, joint mobilization, and meridian-based techniques for pain relief and energy balance.

Moxibustion

Therapeutic heat applied using dried mugwort to acupuncture points to warm the meridians, improve circulation, and support immune function.

Herbal Medicine

Customized herbal formulas prescribed based on your unique TCM diagnosis to support internal healing, balance, and long-term wellness.

What Your Session Includes

A typical TCM session at FUEL unfolds in three interconnected phases:

Initial Intake

Your practitioner takes a comprehensive health history and performs a tongue and pulse assessment — two diagnostic cornerstones of TCM that reveal the state of your organ systems and energy flow.

Acupuncture Treatment

Sterile single-use needles are carefully placed at selected meridian points. Most patients experience minimal sensation and find the 20–40 minute treatment deeply relaxing.

Holistic Adjuncts

Depending on your presentation, your practitioner may incorporate cupping, auricular therapy, Tui Na, or moxibustion to enhance the therapeutic effect of the session.

Benefits of Acupuncture & TCM

Reduce Chronic Pain

Acupuncture activates endorphins and anti-inflammatory pathways — providing lasting relief from musculoskeletal and systemic pain conditions.

Boost Energy

TCM addresses the root causes of fatigue by balancing organ systems, improving sleep quality, and restoring the flow of Qi through the body.

Improve Mental Clarity

Needle placement at specific points calms the nervous system and supports cognitive function, focus, and emotional regulation.

Lower Stress

Acupuncture measurably reduces cortisol levels and activates the parasympathetic nervous system — your body's "rest and digest" state.

In addition to its systemic benefits, acupuncture offers a powerful natural approach to acne and skin health. Our practitioners use a five-step protocol combining multiple TCM modalities:

Frequently Asked Questions

Acupuncture needles are extremely fine — much thinner than a hypodermic needle. Most patients feel little to no pain during insertion. You may notice a dull ache, warmth, or a tingling sensation at the needle site, which practitioners refer to as "De Qi" — a sign that the point has been effectively activated. The majority of patients find the treatment deeply relaxing and often fall asleep during sessions.

Acute conditions typically respond within 4–6 sessions. Chronic conditions may require 8–12 sessions or an ongoing maintenance schedule. TCM is cumulative — effects build over time. Your practitioner will assess your progress after each session and adjust your treatment plan accordingly.

TCM addresses a remarkably broad range of conditions — from musculoskeletal pain and headaches to digestive disorders, fertility challenges, anxiety, insomnia, and skin conditions. The World Health Organization (WHO) recognizes acupuncture as effective for over 100 conditions. During your initial assessment, your TCM practitioner will determine whether TCM is appropriate for your specific presentation.

Many extended health plans in Alberta include acupuncture coverage. FUEL offers direct billing with most major insurers. We recommend confirming your coverage before your first appointment. Our front desk team is happy to help you understand your benefits.
