What Is Manual Osteopathy?

Manual osteopathy is a hands-on, evidence-informed therapy that treats pain, dysfunction, and imbalance throughout the entire body. Rather than focusing on isolated symptoms, osteopathic treatment addresses the root causes — examining how your muscles, joints, fascia, organs, and nervous system work together.

One of the most patient-friendly aspects of osteopathic treatment: patients remain fully clothed throughout the session. Treatment is gentle, precise, and deeply therapeutic — working with the body's own healing mechanisms to restore balance and optimal function.

Osteopathic Treatment Methods

Joint Mobilization

Gentle, rhythmic movements to restore normal range of motion and reduce stiffness in restricted joints throughout the spine and extremities.

Myofascial Release

Targeted techniques to release tension in the fascia — the connective tissue surrounding muscles and organs — improving flexibility and reducing chronic pain patterns.

Visceral Manipulation

Gentle manual techniques applied to the abdomen and thorax to improve the mobility and function of internal organs, often addressing digestive issues, chronic pain, and postural imbalances.

Craniosacral Therapy

Light-touch techniques that work with the rhythmic movement of cerebrospinal fluid to release restrictions in the craniosacral system, reducing headaches, neck tension, and stress.
