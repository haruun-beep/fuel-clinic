Massage Therapy at FUEL

Massage therapy at FUEL goes far beyond relaxation. Our Registered Massage Therapists (RMTs) provide evidence-informed treatment for injury recovery, discomfort management, and preventative care — all within the context of FUEL's interdisciplinary clinical environment.

Our team is trained in a range of therapeutic modalities including traditional therapeutic massage, lymphatic drainage, and Tui Na (Chinese medical massage). This breadth of technique allows us to customize every session to your specific condition, goals, and body.

How Massage Therapy Works

Massage therapy works through the direct manipulation of soft tissues — muscles, tendons, ligaments, and fascia. By targeting these structures with skilled hands-on techniques, your RMT can:

  • Reduce tension and adhesions in muscles and connective tissue
  • Improve local and systemic circulation
  • Stimulate the lymphatic system to reduce inflammation and swelling
  • Activate the parasympathetic nervous system to reduce stress hormones
  • Relieve pain through both mechanical and neurological pathways
  • Improve range of motion and joint flexibility

Massage is a non-invasive, drug-free therapy with an excellent safety profile. It complements every other service offered at FUEL, and many patients incorporate regular massage into their broader treatment plans.

Three Pillars of Massage Therapy at FUEL

Stress Relief

Targeted work on the neck, upper back, and shoulders to release chronic tension. Swedish and deep tissue techniques calm the nervous system and melt away accumulated stress.

Pain Relief

Myofascial release and deep tissue techniques break down adhesions, reduce inflammation, and interrupt pain cycles — delivering lasting relief from chronic and acute pain conditions.

Restoring Function

Targeted work to restore mobility, improve postural alignment, and support healthy sleep patterns. Ideal for rehabilitation and return-to-sport programs.

Massage Therapy Modalities

Therapeutic Massage

Classic Swedish and deep tissue techniques adapted to your therapeutic goals — whether that's relaxation, pain management, or injury rehabilitation.

Lymphatic Drainage

Light, rhythmic strokes that stimulate the lymphatic system, reduce swelling and inflammation, and support immune function. Especially beneficial post-surgery or after injury.

Tui Na (Chinese Medical Massage)

An ancient Chinese therapeutic massage system that works along the body's meridian pathways to balance energy flow, relieve pain, and improve organ function.
