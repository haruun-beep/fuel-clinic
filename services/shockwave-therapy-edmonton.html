Shockwave Therapy at FUEL

FUEL uses the industry-leading EMS Swiss DolorClast® shockwave system — the same technology trusted by professional sports teams and leading rehabilitation clinics worldwide. Extracorporeal Shockwave Therapy (ESWT) delivers high-energy acoustic pressure waves to targeted areas of the body, triggering a cascade of biological responses that accelerate natural healing.

Shockwave therapy is particularly effective for conditions that have not responded adequately to conventional treatments — making it an important option for patients who have been dealing with stubborn, chronic issues.

Three Core Benefits of Shockwave Therapy

Accelerated Healing

Shockwave stimulates blood circulation, promotes angiogenesis (new blood vessel formation), and activates cellular regeneration — dramatically speeding recovery from tendon injuries, muscle tears, and post-surgical tissue.

Pain Relief

Acoustic waves break down calcified deposits and scar tissue, reduce substance P (a pain neurotransmitter), and decrease local inflammation — providing lasting relief from plantar fasciitis, tendonitis, and chronic joint pain.

Performance Recovery

By repairing micro-injuries in muscle and connective tissue and enhancing metabolic activity, shockwave therapy helps athletes recover faster, train harder, and reduce their risk of re-injury.

Are You a Good Candidate for Shockwave?

Shockwave therapy is most effective for patients who:

  • Have chronic muscular or joint discomfort lasting more than 3 months
  • Are dealing with slow-healing sports injuries or overuse conditions
  • Suffer from plantar fasciitis, Achilles tendonopathy, or patellar tendonitis
  • Have tried other treatments (massage, chiropractic, physio) with limited results
  • Want to avoid steroid injections or surgical intervention
  • Are looking to accelerate recovery and return to activity

Low Level Laser Therapy (LLLT)

FUEL also offers LuxMaster Low Level Laser Therapy (LLLT) — a targeted photobiomodulation technology that uses specific low-intensity light wavelengths to penetrate tissue and stimulate cellular healing at the mitochondrial level.

Unlike shockwave, LLLT is completely painless and non-thermal. It works by delivering photons into damaged tissue, stimulating ATP production, reducing oxidative stress, and activating anti-inflammatory pathways. Sessions are quick, comfortable, and highly effective for a range of acute and chronic conditions.

Frequently Asked Questions

Shockwave therapy can cause mild to moderate discomfort during treatment, particularly over inflamed or sensitive areas. Most patients describe it as a deep, rapid tapping sensation. Treatment intensity is always adjusted to your tolerance, and discomfort during the session is generally considered a positive sign that the therapy is reaching the targeted tissue. Post-treatment soreness for 24–48 hours is normal and indicates the healing response has been activated.

Most patients see significant improvement within 3–5 sessions, typically spaced one week apart. Chronic conditions may require up to 6–8 sessions. Many patients notice improvement after their very first session. Your practitioner will assess your response and adjust the treatment plan accordingly. Results continue to develop for 6–12 weeks after a course of treatment as the tissue continues to remodel.

Low Level Laser Therapy (LLLT) uses specific wavelengths of low-intensity light to stimulate cellular healing — it is completely painless and non-thermal. Shockwave uses high-energy acoustic pressure waves and involves some degree of treatment sensation. LLLT works best for reducing inflammation and promoting healing in acute and subacute conditions; shockwave excels at breaking down chronic tissue changes (calcifications, fibrosis) and stimulating healing in stubborn tendinopathies. The two therapies can be combined for enhanced outcomes.

Coverage varies by insurance plan. Some extended health plans include shockwave or laser therapy under physiotherapy or other allied health benefits. FUEL offers direct billing where applicable. We recommend contacting your insurer prior to your appointment to confirm your coverage details.
